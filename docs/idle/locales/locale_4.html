Rising above the encroaching forest canopy, the Verdigris Observatory is a forgotten remnant of arcane stargazing, its domed summit now cloaked in green-streaked copper and trailing ivy. Once a marvel of precision engineering and magical astronomy, the tower now hums with a quiet, uncanny tension — as if time itself were holding its breath.

Within its chambers, the air shimmers with an unseen pressure. Brass rings of shattered armillary spheres turn slowly without touch, their runes flickering dimly with residual starlight. Moss-covered stone steps wind past walls lined with celestial charts now faded and warped, their constellations whispering to those who dare study them too closely.

The copper devices, once polished to a mirror shine, are now oxidized and dulled — save for the occasional gleam, as if in defiance of their age.

The observatory is far from lifeless.

Outside, the ruins of satellite observatories peek from the underbrush, and strange glyphs bloom across stones at night. Atop the highest dome, the great telescope — now frozen in place — points unwaveringly at a single patch of sky, where no star has shone for a thousand years.

To stand at the heart of Verdigris Observatory is to feel watched — not by any living being, but by the cosmos itself.

Inhabitants

Armillary Constructs

Forged of brass and arcane energy, drift silently through the halls, continuing their measurements and calibrations long after their creators vanished. They seem unaware — or perhaps unconcerned — with the passage of time or intruding visitors.

Rust Beetle

Scuttle across metal fixtures and collapsed scaffolding, drawn to the oxidized copper like moths to flame. Their carapaces glint with patina, and their gnawing mandibles are strong enough to weaken even enchanted alloys.

Wandering Scholar

Phantoms of those who once studied here, pace the upper galleries murmuring forgotten theories. Their ghostly forms flicker with each step, trailing ink-blot shadows and wisps of torn parchment.

Arcane Wisps

Drift like motes of light caught in gravitational eddies, attracted to any source of magical energy. They spark with celestial hues and may assist or hinder explorers, depending on their chaotic whims.

Verdant Slime

Seep from cracked stone and overgrown roots, absorbing ambient magic and nutrients from the decaying tower. Slow and ponderous, they glow faintly with bioluminescence and leave trails of creeping vines in their wake.

Clockwork Scribe

Spider-like automatons etched with glowing runes, patrol the lower archives, endlessly transcribing illegible glyphs into decaying tomes. Some say they record the thoughts of the stars themselves.
